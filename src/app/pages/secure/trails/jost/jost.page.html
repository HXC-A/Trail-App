The Jeffrey Open Space Trail (JOST), the product of extensive community input and consensus, is a unique resource for the residents of the City of Irvine.
This open space corridor constitutes an important element in the City's overall open space system, linking the conservation and open space lands within the City.
The trail currently stretches 3.5 miles through the City of Irvine.
The 1.5-mile extension will run from Barranca to I-5, and will link existing bike and pedestrian trails, include a 15-footwide bridge stretching almost
1,200 feet over the Interstate 5, and connect the coastline to the Santa Ana Mountains and Cleveland National Forest.
